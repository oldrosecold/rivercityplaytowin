Rating Guide:
8.0+ = Excellent • 7.5-7.9 = Very Good • 7.0-7.4 = Good

Fateforge: Chronicles of Kaan

8.26
Players: 1-4 (Best: 1-2) Time: 60 min Weight: 2.8/5

A cooperative narrative action-adventure game designed by Gordon Calleja. Players explore an evolving story using dice rolling, action retrieval, and modular board mechanics. At medium complexity (2.8), it's accessible to newcomers while offering depth for experienced players. Sessions can be broken into episodes under an hour. Theme involves fantasy adventure in the world of Kaan.

Hot Streak

8.01
Players: 2-9 (Community: 3-8, Best: 5-6) Time: 20 min Weight: 1.2/5

A very light party racing game designed by Jon Perry featuring betting, bluffing, and open drafting. At weight 1.2, rules can be memorized quickly or fit on a small crib sheet. Plays in just 20 minutes with flexible player counts from 3-8. Best experienced with 5-6 players for optimal interaction. Mascots race to the finish line with potential disqualifications adding drama.

Magical Athlete

7.95
Players: 2-6 (Best: 5-6) Time: 30 min Weight: 1.2/5

A racing game co-designed by Richard Garfield (creator of Magic: The Gathering) and Takashi Ishida. Features open drafting, roll-and-move, and variable player powers as characters race to the finish. Extremely light at 1.2 weight, perfect for families or casual groups. Best with 5-6 players where character interactions shine. Plays in about 30 minutes with crazy character abilities creating memorable moments.

Shallow Sea

7.92
Players: 1-4 (Community: 1-3, Best: 2) Time: 30-45 min Weight: 2.3/5

A multi-layer puzzle game by Yeom.C" featuring marine animals of the Great Barrier Reef. Uses pattern building, set collection, and grid coverage mechanics at a light-medium weight (2.3). Works well solo or with 2 players, with 2-player being the sweet spot. Includes open drafting and end-game bonuses for strategic depth. Theme focuses on ocean life and reef ecosystems.

Beyond the Sun

7.87
Players: 2-4 (Best: 3) Time: 60-120 min Weight: 3.2/5

A medium-heavy strategy game by Dennis K. Chan where players collectively develop a tech tree to colonize space. Features area majority, tableau building, and a unique player-driven technology system. At weight 3.2, it offers substantial strategic depth while remaining accessible. Best at 3 players with 17,000+ ratings attesting to its popularity. Plan for 90-120 minutes for a full session.

Monikers

7.77
Players: 4-16 (Community: 4-12, Best: 6-10) Time: 30-60 min Weight: 1.1/5

A team-based party game by Alex Hague and Justin Vickers where players guess names with different rules each round. Extremely light (1.1 weight) with acting, role playing, and communication limits across three rounds. Scales wonderfully from 6 to 10 players where it's at its best. Perfect for large groups and parties. Over 7,500 ratings make it a proven crowd-pleaser.

Rolling Realms Redux

7.76
Players: 1-6 (Community: 1-5, Best: 1-2) Time: 30-45 min Weight: 2.6/5

A roll-and-write game by Jamey Stegmaier (Stonemaier Games) with Karel Titeca. Features simultaneous action selection and references to other Stonemaier games. At 2.6 weight, it's meatier than typical roll-and-writes. Best as a solo or 2-player experience. Includes solo design by Morten Monrad Pedersen (Automa Factory).

Lunar Rush

7.63
Players: 1-4 (Best: 4) Time: 45-75 min Weight: 2.9/5

An economic game by Steven "Skippy" Brown about corporations racing to mine the Moon. Features auction/bidding, market manipulation, and simultaneous action selection. Medium complexity (2.9) with strategic depth. Best with 4 players for maximum market competition. Works well solo with dedicated solitaire mode.

Woodcraft

7.58
Players: 1-4 (Best: 3) Time: 60-120 min Weight: 3.8/5

A heavier euro game by Ross Arnold and Vladimír Suchý (designer of Underwater Cities). Features action drafting, rondel, dice rolling, and contract fulfillment. At weight 3.8, this is one of the more complex games on the list. Best at 3 players according to community. Players grow trees, gather materials, and craft items to build the best workshop.

Rock Hard: 1977

7.51
Players: 2-5 (Best: 4-5) Time: 45-90 min Weight: 2.5/5

A worker placement game by Jackie Fox about becoming a rock star in 1977. Features set collection, push your luck, and variable player powers. Medium complexity (2.5) makes it accessible while offering meaningful decisions. Best with 4-5 players. Rehearse, play gigs, and pursue record deals in this thematic experience.

Evacuation

7.51
Players: 1-4 (Best: 2-3) Time: 60-150 min Weight: 4.0/5

A heavy strategy game by Vladimír Suchý (designer of Underwater Cities, Woodcraft). At weight 4.0, this is the most complex game on the list. Features hand management, pick-up-and-deliver, and income mechanics. Best at 2-3 players where downtime is manageable. Plan for 2+ hours for a full game. Theme involves evacuating a planet before it burns up.

Dice Words

7.47
Players: 2-4 (Best: 2) Time: 30 min Weight: N/A

A word game by Tim Phillips using letter dice to form valuable words. Features dice rolling with re-roll mechanics and target letter bonuses. Best as a 2-player experience. Quick 30-minute playtime makes it easy to fit into any game session. Appeals to fans of word games like Scrabble or Boggle.

First in Flight

7.45
Players: 1-4 (Best: 3) Time: 45-75 min Weight: 2.3/5

A push-your-luck racing game about early aviation by Matthew O'Malley and Ben Rosset. Features deck building, rondel, time track, and variable player powers. Light-medium weight (2.3) makes it accessible. Best at 3 players per community. Includes solo mode by Richard Wilkins.

Furnace

An engine-building auction game by Ivan Lashin. Features unique auction compensation mechanic where losing bids still provide benefits. Light-medium weight (2.3) with nearly 13,000 ratings. Best at 3-4 players where auction tension peaks. 19th century industrial capitalism theme.

Lacuna

7.36
Players: 2 only (Best: 2) Time: 10-15 min Weight: 1.1/5

A 2-player abstract by Mark Gerrits from CMYK. Extremely light (1.1) with games lasting just 10-15 minutes. Uses zone of control mechanics to collect flowers and block opponents. Perfect filler for two players. Over 2,200 ratings with beautiful flower theme.

Ecosystem: Savanna

A card drafting game by Daniel Dávalos and team about Serengeti wildlife. Features closed drafting, pattern building, and set collection. Light-medium weight (2.0) with very quick 15-20 minute playtime. Scales from solo to 6 players with solo being optimal. Great for teaching drafting concepts.

Ham Helsing

7.34
Players: 1-4 (Best: 4) Time: 60-90 min Weight: 2.7/5

A cooperative game by Justin De Witt featuring dice rolling and deck building. Medium complexity (2.7) with longer 60-90 minute playtime. Best at 4 players for full team experience. Features events and layering mechanics. Theme involves pig heroes saving Mud Canyon from supernatural threats.

Draft & Write Records

7.31

Kingdomino

7.29
Players: 2-4 (Best: 2, 4) Time: 15-25 min Weight: 1.2/5

A Spiel des Jahres winner by Bruno Cathala (designer of 7 Wonders Duel). Features tile placement and open drafting with clever turn order mechanism. Very light (1.2) and quick at 15-25 minutes. Over 52,000 ratings make it one of the most popular games ever. Best at 2 or 4 players. Perfect gateway game.
